Financial Risk Mitigation & Audit Compliance

A controls-focused review that catches billing, revenue, and data integrity risks before they become audit findings.

Scenario

Finance needs proof that revenue processes are complete, accurate, and traceable across systems.

Outcome

Makes risk visible early and gives teams a cleaner path to remediation.

Real-World Business Example

A company discovers that manual billing adjustments can be entered without a consistent approval trail. I would review adjustment tables, user roles, timestamps, approval notes, and invoice outcomes to find gaps in the control process. Then I would create an exception report for adjustments over a defined threshold, missing approval metadata, or repeated adjustments by the same user and period.

  • Define the control requirement in plain business terms: who can adjust revenue, when, and why.
  • Create audit checks that flag missing approvals, unusual amounts, and repeated corrections.
  • Produce evidence that finance can use for remediation and audit support.

How I Apply It

  1. Compare control expectations against current system behavior.
  2. Create exception checks for missing approvals, stale data, and unusual adjustments.
  3. Capture evidence in a format audit stakeholders can follow.
